Statistics

Unless we cut 7% of carbon emissions, there will be a 1.5 to 5 degrees Celsius rise in global average temperature by 2100 and a 34 cm rise in sea levels by 2050.

  • The current concentration of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere is the highest in human history: 416 parts per million.
  • 2019 was the second warmest year on record - the five warmest have all been since 2015.
  • 11% of emissions are caused by deforestation.

    • What does all of this mean? We can do something about climate change! GreenBook offers resources to get started living a more sustainable life, as well as advocating for green solutions to climate change at the local, national, and global levels.
